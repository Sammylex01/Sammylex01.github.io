Albert Einstein

1879 - 1955

One of the greatest scientist in the 18th and 19th Century

Albert Einstein (/ˈaɪnstaɪn/ EYEN-styne; 14 March 1879 – 18 April 1955) was a German-born theoretical physicist[5] who developed the theory of relativity, one of the two pillars of modern physics (alongside quantum mechanics).[3][6]:274 His work is also known for its influence on the philosophy of science.[7][8] He is best known to the general public for his mass–energy equivalence formula E = mc2, which has been dubbed "the world's most famous equation".
